15. Body-worn cameras
WarmRight engineers may wear body-worn cameras during property visits.
Unless otherwise agreed or decided in the circumstances, the camera may record video and audio throughout the visit rather than being activated only after an incident begins.
Whole-visit recording is used because:
•	activating a camera only after a disagreement begins may escalate the situation;
•	an engineer may not know in advance which part of a visit will later become relevant;
•	complete footage can provide context that a short recording may not show;
•	it can verify the condition of an installation and the work completed;
•	it can help protect customers and engineers;
•	it can assist with safety incidents, complaints, liability disputes and legal claims.
Body-worn cameras are not used for general advertising or public entertainment.
Customers should be informed about body-worn recording before or at the beginning of the visit. The camera will normally be visible on the engineer.
Recordings may include audio because conversations, instructions, warnings and explanations can be relevant to establishing what occurred during a visit.
Engineers should avoid deliberately recording people, rooms, documents or activities that are unrelated to the work where reasonably possible.
Objecting to body-worn recording
You may object to the use of a body-worn camera.
The engineer or WarmRight will consider the circumstances, including:
•	the reason for the objection;
•	the nature of the work;
•	previous incidents or disputes;
•	risks to the customer or engineer;
•	the need to document the work;
•	whether recording can reasonably be limited or stopped.
Depending on the circumstances, the engineer may:
•	turn off the camera and continue;
•	limit the recording;
•	contact the office for guidance;
•	postpone the appointment;
•	decline to enter or continue working.
Where an engineer reasonably considers recording necessary for personal safety, accurate evidence or liability protection, WarmRight may decide that the visit cannot proceed without recording.
This does not remove any statutory rights the customer may have.

20. How long we keep information
We do not keep personal information indefinitely merely because it may be useful in the future.
Our normal retention periods are:
Customer, job and financial records
Customer account information, completed job records, photographs relating to the work, certificates, quotations, invoices and payment records are normally retained for up to seven years.
They may be kept longer where required by law, an active warranty, a continuing contract, an unresolved complaint or a legal claim.
Enquiries that do not become jobs
Enquiries that do not result in work are normally retained for up to 18 months.
Call recordings
Routine call recordings are normally retained for up to 12 months.
A recording may be retained longer where it is relevant to:
•	an active complaint;
•	a payment dispute;
•	a safety incident;
•	threatened or actual legal proceedings;
•	an insurance claim;
•	an investigation.
Body-worn camera footage
Routine body-worn camera footage is normally retained for up to 24 months.
WarmRight considers this period appropriate because complaints, property issues and allegations relating to completed work may arise some time after the visit.
Footage connected with an incident, complaint, insurance matter or legal claim may be retained for longer. Where necessary, it may be retained until the matter is resolved and for an appropriate period afterwards, potentially up to seven years where needed for legal claims.
Unpublished feedback
Feedback that is not published is normally retained for up to 12 months, unless it is relevant to a complaint, customer record or ongoing matter.
Published testimonials
Published testimonials and photographs are retained while they remain in use or until permission is withdrawn.
Records showing that publication permission was given may be retained afterwards where reasonably necessary to demonstrate that WarmRight had authority to use the material.
Analytics
Website analytics records are normally retained for up to 24 months, unless anonymised or aggregated so they no longer identify or relate to an individual.
Vulnerability information
Health, disability and vulnerability information is kept only for as long as it remains relevant to the customer relationship, an active job, safety requirement or legal claim.
Retention periods may be shortened or extended where reasonably necessary, but they are reviewed against the purpose for which the information was collected.
